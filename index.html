Phase I (1954–September 1956)


1. 1954 - FLN Launches the Insurgency Summary: The FLN began with a series of bombings and guerrilla attacks on French security forces. Interpretation: This marked the start of the war for Algerian independence, challenging French colonial control.

2. 1955 - Philippeville Massacre Summary: The FLN killed 123 civilians, prompting harsh reprisals where over 12,000 Algerians were killed. Interpretation: French collective punishment fueled resentment, pushing more Algerians toward supporting the FLN.

3. 1956 - FLN Establishes External Support Summary: Tunisia and Morocco provided safe havens, while Egypt and Syria supplied arms and training. Interpretation: This international support strengthened the FLN's military capabilities.

4. 1956 - FLN Structures Parallel Government Summary: The FLN formed military and civil committees to govern areas under their control. Interpretation: The insurgents created a competing authority to undermine French rule.

Phase II (September 1956–1957)


5. 1956 - FLN Begins Urban Terrorism in Algiers Summary: The FLN bombed civilian areas to provoke French overreactions. Interpretation: The tactic aimed to erode French legitimacy and gain international attention.

6. 1957 - Battle for Algiers Begins Summary: The French military responded with brutal tactics, including torture and mass detentions. Interpretation: While militarily effective, these methods alienated the local population and global opinion.

7. 1957 - French Build Quadrillage System Summary: The French divided Algeria into sectors, stationing troops to restrict FLN movement. Interpretation: This strategy hindered FLN operations but displaced over a million Algerians.

8. 1957 - Morice Line Constructed Summary: A border fence with Tunisia was built to cut FLN supply lines. Interpretation: The barrier effectively reduced cross-border insurgent activities.

Phase III (1958–1959)


9. 1958 - De Gaulle Returns to Power Summary: Charles de Gaulle became president and implemented both military and political reforms. Interpretation: His leadership aimed to stabilize France and resolve the Algerian conflict.

10. 1958 - FLN Establishes Government-in-Exile Summary: The FLN formed the Provisional Government of the Algerian Republic in Tunis. Interpretation: This move garnered international recognition for the independence movement.

11. 1959 - French Military Gains Tactical Victories Summary: Sweeping operations and mobile units dismantled FLN bases. Interpretation: Despite military successes, political support for the war waned.

12. 1959 - De Gaulle Promises Self-Determination Summary: De Gaulle announced a referendum on Algerian autonomy within four years. Interpretation: This concession reflected growing pressure for a political solution.

Phase IV (1960–1962)


13. 1960 - Barricade WeekSummary: French settlers rioted in opposition to De Gaulle's policies, creating blockades and firing on troops.Interpretation: The radical colons highlighted divisions within French society over Algeria.

14. 1961 - OAS FormedSummary: The Organization of the Secret Army launched terrorist attacks against Algerians and French officials.Interpretation: This extremist response failed to garner public support and alienated even some loyalists.

15. 1961 - Failed Coup Against De GaulleSummary: French generals attempted a coup to prevent Algerian independence but lacked support.Interpretation: De Gaulle's leadership remained strong despite internal opposition.

16. 1962 - Evian Accords SignedSummary: The French government and FLN agreed on Algerian independence terms.Interpretation: The accords ended eight years of conflict, marking France's political defeat.

17. 1962 - Algerian ReferendumSummary: A referendum overwhelmingly voted for independence.Interpretation: This solidified Algeria’s status as a sovereign state.

18. 1962 - French WithdrawalSummary: French forces departed, and Algeria became independent in July.Interpretation: The war demonstrated the decline of colonialism in the post-World War II era.

19. 1962 - OAS Violence PeaksSummary: The OAS escalated its attacks but failed to stop the independence process.Interpretation: Their efforts reflected the desperation of the settler and military extremists.

20. 1962 - Aftermath of IndependenceSummary: Up to one million Algerians died, with deep societal scars on both sides.Interpretation: The war's brutality underscored the costs of decolonization.
