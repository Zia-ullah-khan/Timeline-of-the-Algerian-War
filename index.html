PPhase II: Methods (1920-1947)


5. 1956 - FLN Begins Urban Terrorism in Algiers Summary: The FLN bombed civilian areas to provoke French overreactions. Interpretation: The tactic aimed to erode French legitimacy and gain international attention.

6. 1957 - Battle for Algiers Begins Summary: The French military responded with brutal tactics, including torture and mass detentions. Interpretation: While militarily effective, these methods alienated the local population and global opinion.

7. 1957 - French Build Quadrillage System Summary: The French divided Algeria into sectors, stationing troops to restrict FLN movement. Interpretation: This strategy hindered FLN operations but displaced over a million Algerians.

8. 1957 - Morice Line Constructed Summary: A border fence with Tunisia was built to cut FLN supply lines. Interpretation: The barrier effectively reduced cross-border insurgent activities.

Phase III (1958–1959)


9. 1958 - De Gaulle Returns to Power Summary: Charles de Gaulle became president and implemented both military and political reforms. Interpretation: His leadership aimed to stabilize France and resolve the Algerian conflict.

10. 1958 - FLN Establishes Government-in-Exile Summary: The FLN formed the Provisional Government of the Algerian Republic in Tunis. Interpretation: This move garnered international recognition for the independence movement.

11. 1959 - French Military Gains Tactical Victories Summary: Sweeping operations and mobile units dismantled FLN bases. Interpretation: Despite military successes, political support for the war waned.

12. 1959 - De Gaulle Promises Self-Determination Summary: De Gaulle announced a referendum on Algerian autonomy within four years. Interpretation: This concession reflected growing pressure for a political solution.
